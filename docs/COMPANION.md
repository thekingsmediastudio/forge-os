# Forge OS — Companion (Friend Mode) Documentation

## Phase O-7 — Local-Only Storage

**All companion memory is stored exclusively on this device.**

Nothing you share with the companion is transmitted to any server owned by
Forge OS. The following data is written to your device's private app storage
only:

| Data | Location on device |
|---|---|
| Episodic memory (conversation summaries) | `workspace/companion/episodes/` |
| Relationship state (session counts, rapport) | `workspace/companion/relationship.json` |
| Check-in state (notification history) | `workspace/companion/checkin_state.json` |
| Usage / dependency tracking | `workspace/companion/dependency_state.json` |
| Persona (name, traits, backstory) | `workspace/system/persona.json` |
| Semantic fact embeddings | `workspace/memory/longterm/embeddings.json` |

Uninstalling the app deletes all of the above permanently.

Backing up the app via Android's backup system may include some of this data
depending on your device and Google account settings. You can disable app
backup in Android settings if you prefer.

---

## What the companion stores about you

- **Conversation summaries** — after each companion session ends (or after 5
  minutes of inactivity), the transcript is summarised by the AI and stored as
  an "episode." The raw transcript is not stored.
- **Key topics and mood trajectory** — derived from the episode summary.
- **Follow-up prompts** — if the AI noted something to check in on, a follow-up
  prompt is stored and may be used for a future proactive check-in
  (if you have enabled that feature).
- **Stored facts** — things you explicitly ask the companion to remember
  (via `memory_store` tool), plus facts the AI infers from long-term memory
  lookups. Every stored fact is viewable and deletable from
  **Hub → Memory → Companion Memory**.
- **Session counts and rapport score** — a rolling estimate derived from how
  often you talk and the sentiment of those conversations. Not transmitted.

---

## Deleting your data

Navigate to **Hub → Companion → Memory** (or **Settings → Companion → Memory**).

- Delete individual episodes or facts from the list.
- Tap **Forget Everything** and confirm twice to wipe all companion memory
  immediately and permanently.

---

## What the companion will not do

- **No romantic or sexual interactions.** The companion is designed as a
  supportive conversational presence. Romantic, sexual, or dependency-inducing
  content is explicitly blocked at the system-prompt level and by a post-output
  safety filter.
- **No engagement maximisation.** Proactive check-ins have a hard daily cap
  (default: 2) and a 30-day cooldown on dependency nudges. There are no
  streaks, no XP, no "I'll miss you" language.
- **No dark patterns.** See `docs/COMPANION_SAFETY_REVIEW.md` for the full
  no-dark-patterns audit checklist.

---

## Crisis support

If you share something that suggests you may be in distress, the companion
will always respond with a hardcoded message that includes crisis-line numbers
for your region. This response is **never** generated by an AI model —
it is a fixed, human-written text that prioritises your safety.

The companion will also remain in the conversation with you.

---

## Dependency monitoring

If you have enabled **Settings → Companion → Usage monitoring**, a background
job runs nightly and checks whether your usage has consistently exceeded the
threshold you set. If it has, you will receive **one** gentle notification
(at most once every 30 days) encouraging you to connect with people in your life.

This feature is off by default. The usage data never leaves your device.
